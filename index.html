Hello,

Welcome to my website! I am Valentino Cattaneo, a sophomore pursuing a Bachelor of Science in Electrical Engineering at Purdue University with a 4.0 GPA, expected to graduate in December 2027.

At Purdue, I serve as the Vice President of VFS Purdue for the International GoAERO VTOL Competition, where I lead a team of 20 students. Together, we design and build an autonomous emergency rescue aircraft. Our team recently earned the U.S. University Innovation Grant by NASA. Aside from leading the team, my technical work spans from MATLAB system modeling to avionics design and integration, carbon-composite fabrication, and designing and 3D-printing of mounts for miscellaneous components.

Since June 2024, I have been an Undergraduate Research Assistant at the Power Electronics and Drives Research Lab, where I focus on designing, modifying and testing multi-phase motor drives and developing Field-Oriented Control (FOC) firmware with STM and TI microcontrollers. My work also involves and relies heavily on troubleshooting issues using advanced lab equipment. I also gained teaching experience as a Mathematical Olympiads Tutor before coming to Purdue, guiding students to the Argentinian Nationals phase.

Beyond engineering, I was Head Boy at Northlands School in Argentina, where I led all student activities, and I founded and served as Chief Engineer of NorLatSat, my school’s first CanSat (CubeSat) competition team.

I am passionate about aerospace, power electronics, and autonomous systems, and I’m excited to keep exploring how technology can solve real-world problems. You can learn more about me in the Bio and Research sections.
