Hello,

Welcome to my website! I am Valentino Cattaneo, a sophomore pursuing a Bachelor of Science in Electrical Engineering at Purdue University with a 4.0 GPA, expected to graduate in December 2027.

At Purdue, I serve as the Vice President of VFS Purdue for the International GoAERO VTOL Competition, where I lead a team of 20 students. Together, we design and build an autonomous emergency rescue aircraft. Our team recently earned the U.S. University Innovation Grant by NASA. My work spans from MATLAB system modeling to avionics integration, carbon-composite fabrication, and 3D-printed components for prototyping.

Since June 2024, I have been an Undergraduate Research Assistant in the Purdue ECE Department, modifying and testing multi-phase motor drives, developing Field-Oriented Control (FOC) firmware, and troubleshooting using advanced instrumentation. I also gained teaching experience as a Mathematical Olympiads Tutor, guiding students to the Nationals phase in Argentina.

Beyond engineering, I was Head Boy at Northlands School in Argentina, where I led all student activities, and I founded and served as Chief Engineer of NorLatSat, my school’s first CubeSat competition team.

I am passionate about aerospace, power electronics, and autonomous systems, and I’m excited to keep exploring how technology can solve real-world problems. You can learn more in the Bio and Research sections.
