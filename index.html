Hello,

Welcome to my website! I am Valentino Cattaneo, a sophomore pursuing a Bachelor of Science in Electrical Engineering at Purdue University with a 4.0 GPA, expected to graduate in December 2027.

At Purdue, I serve as the President of VFS Purdue for the International GoAERO VTOL Competition, where I lead a team of ~20 Engineering students. Together, we design and build an autonomous emergency rescue aircraft. Our team recently earned the U.S. University Innovation Award by NASA in Stage 1 of the Competition. As part of Stage 2, we designed, constructed, and autonomously flown a 40% scaled-down prototype. My technical work in the prototype consisted of MATLAB system modeling, avionics and powertrain sizing, wiring design, and integration. For Stage 3 of the competition, I am working closely with the Powertrain lead to design our 400V battery and our Battery Management System.

Since June 2025, I have been an Undergraduate Research Assistant at the Power Electronics and Drives Research Lab, where I focus on designing, modifying and testing multi-phase motor drives and developing Sensored Field-Oriented Control (FOC) firmware with STM and TI microcontrollers. My work also involves and relies heavily on troubleshooting issues using advanced lab equipment. I also gained teaching experience as a Mathematical Olympiads Tutor before coming to Purdue, guiding students to the Argentinian Nationals phase.

Before coming to Purdue, I was Head Boy at Northlands School in Argentina, where I led all student activities, and I founded and served as Chief Engineer of NorLatSat, my school’s CanSat (CubeSat-like) competition team.

I am passionate about aerospace, power electronics, and autonomous systems, and I’m excited to keep exploring how technology can solve real-world problems. You can learn more about me in the Bio and Research sections.
